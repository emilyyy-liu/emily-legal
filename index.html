Workflow — general business sale/purchase

Adapt per matter type above. Check whether a lease is included — this determines which subfolder applies.

1

Intake & conflict check

Foundation
  • Run conflict check — confirm no existing client on the other side before accepting instructions
  • Obtain client ID / AML documents; confirm who gives instructions (individual, company, trustee)
  • Issue cost agreement and retainer; obtain signed authority to proceed
  • Confirm applicable contract edition (2021 NSW Contract for Sale & Purchase of Business)
  • Determine: does transaction include a lease? → select correct Drive subfolder
📁 Business & Lease folder
2

Due diligence & contract review

Investigation
  • Review contract schedules — employees, plant & equipment, goodwill, IP, trading stock
  • PPSR search — identify registered security interests; obtain release obligations
  • ASIC / ABN / company search on vendor and purchaser entity
  • Review lease — landlord consent required? Assignment conditions? Rent review mechanism?
  • Review franchisor arrangements — consent and transfer conditions if franchise
  • Identify key person / restraint of trade obligations — restraint deeds required?
  • Advise client on outstanding information; negotiate special conditions as required
📁 Purchase (incl. lease) Purchase (excl. lease) Sale of Business
3

Pre-settlement preparation

Execution
  • Confirm satisfaction of all conditions precedent (finance, due diligence, consents)
  • Obtain all required consents — landlord, franchisor, licensor
  • Prepare or review restraint deed, novation agreements, employee letters
  • PPSR discharge — confirm release obtained prior to settlement
  • Prepare settlement agenda; confirm figures and obligations with other solicitor
  • Arrange trading stock adjustment / stocktake procedure if required
  • Confirm transfer of domain, website, social media, phone numbers, utilities
📁 Prepare for Settlement Deed of Exclusivity Licence Agreement
4

Settlement & file closure

Completion
  • Attend settlement — exchange documents, confirm funds received/paid
  • Release deposit to vendor upon settlement (if held in trust)
  • Register any PPSR discharge or new security interests post-settlement
  • Notify relevant parties — ATO, employees, franchisor, utilities
  • Post-settlement letter to client; issue final invoice; close file

Workflow — commercial loan
1

Instructions & terms review

Foundation
  • Conflict check; obtain instructions from lender or borrower; confirm role
  • Review loan term sheet — principal, interest rate, term, repayment schedule, security
  • Identify security package — mortgage over land, PPSR security interest, personal guarantee
  • Advise client on terms; negotiate any amendments to the draft facility agreement
2

Drafting & execution

Drafting
  • Draft or review facility agreement; prepare ancillary security documents
  • If mortgage — coordinate with conveyancing team; prepare mortgage instrument
  • Prepare and register PPSR security interest (PMSI or general security agreement)
  • Prepare guarantee documentation; advise guarantor independently where required
  • Arrange execution by all parties; attend to any witnessing requirements
📁 Commercial Loan folder
3

Registration & drawdown

Completion
  • Lodge PPSR financing statement within required timeframe (24–72 hrs from signing)
  • Confirm registration of mortgage at Land Registry Services if real property security
  • Confirm drawdown conditions satisfied; notify lender to release funds
  • Report to client; issue final invoice; retain loan documents on file
Workflow — family / unit trust
1

Structuring advice & instructions

Foundation
  • Conflict check; obtain instructions; clarify purpose of trust (asset protection, estate planning, investment)
  • Confirm structure: discretionary (family) trust or unit trust — different tax and control implications
  • Identify trustee: individual, company (preferred for liability protection); confirm if new company needs to be set up
  • Refer to accountant / tax advisor for stamp duty and CGT advice on transfer of assets into trust
2

Drafting trust deed

Drafting
  • Draft trust deed — settlor, trustee, beneficiaries, vesting date, trustee powers, income distribution
  • For unit trust: define units, unit holders, unit transfer restrictions, redemption mechanism
  • Advise trustee on duties: duty to account, act impartially, invest prudently
  • Prepare any accompanying corporate trustee documents (shareholders agreement, director resolutions)
📁 Family / Unit Trust folder
3

Execution & registration

Completion
  • Execute trust deed — settlor signs; trustee executes and dates after settlor
  • Pay stamp duty on trust deed if applicable in NSW (currently $500 nominal for discretionary trusts)
  • Apply for TFN and ABN for trust; open trust bank account
  • Register for GST if required; advise client on ongoing trust administration obligations
Workflow — ABN / ACN registration
1

Instructions & entity confirmation

Foundation
  • Confirm entity type: sole trader (ABN only), company (ACN + ABN), trust, partnership
  • Confirm business activity and business name registration requirements
  • Obtain director / member details, date of birth, TFN, residential address for ASIC lodgement
2

Registration & completion

Completion
  • ACN: Lodge Form 201 (Incorporating a Company) with ASIC via ASIC Connect; pay lodgement fee
  • Obtain certificate of incorporation and ACN from ASIC
  • ABN: Apply via Australian Business Register (ABR); link ABN to ACN for companies
  • Register for GST if turnover expected to exceed $75,000; consider PAYG withholding registration
  • Confirm business name registration via ASIC if trading under a name other than legal name
  • Report to client; forward all certificates and registration confirmations

Workflow — international sale of goods
1

Instructions & jurisdiction check

Foundation
  • Confirm parties: Australian exporter/importer and foreign counterpart; confirm governing law clause (prefer NSW)
  • Check if CISG (UN Convention on Contracts for International Sale of Goods) applies — exclude expressly if not wanted
  • Identify Incoterms rule applicable — FOB, CIF, DAP, DDP etc. — determines risk transfer and cost allocation
  • Review import/export controls — AUSTRADE, DFAT permits, sanctions compliance
2

Drafting the contract

Drafting
  • Draft or review international sale contract — description of goods, price, payment terms (LC, TT, open account)
  • Include force majeure, dispute resolution (SIAC arbitration preferred for Asia-Pacific), and confidentiality clauses
  • Payment security: letter of credit — advise on documentary requirements and strict compliance rule
  • Insurance: confirm cargo insurance obligations under chosen Incoterms
📁 Incoterms diagram
3

Execution & shipment

Completion
  • Coordinate execution — electronic or wet ink depending on jurisdiction; apostille if required
  • Confirm shipping documentation: bill of lading, commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin
  • Monitor payment — confirm LC presented and complying documents tendered within time
  • Advise on dispute resolution if goods rejected, delayed, or payment refused

Workflow — Caveat
1

Instructions & caveatable interest

Foundation
  • Confirm client has a caveatable interest — equitable interest (unpaid vendor, constructive trust, equitable charge)
  • Warn client: lodging a caveat without reasonable cause attracts liability in damages (s74P Real Property Act 1900 NSW)
  • Identify the property on title; obtain CT reference and registered proprietor's name
2

Lodgement & monitoring

Lodgement
  • Prepare caveat — identify caveatable interest precisely, extent of prohibition, caveator's address for service
  • Lodge via PEXA (electronic) or NSW LRS; pay lodgement fee
  • Lapsing notice: if served, client has 21 days to obtain court order extending caveat or caveat lapses — diarise immediately
  • Advise client to pursue underlying claim; caveat is protective only, not a substitute for proceedings

Workflow — Deed
1

Drafting & execution

Drafting
  • Identify why a deed is required — consideration not given, deed required by statute, or for added enforceability
  • Draft deed — clear recitals, operative clauses, execution block (signed, sealed, delivered); no bare agreement language
  • Execution: each party signs before a witness who is present; witness signs and provides address
  • Company execution: s127 Corporations Act — two directors, or one director + company secretary, or sole director/secretary; no witness required for s127
  • Retain original deed on file; provide certified copy to client

Workflow — Employment Agreement (bespoke)
1

Instructions & legislative compliance

Foundation
  • Confirm applicable industrial instrument — Modern Award (which award?), Enterprise Agreement, or award-free
  • Confirm classification, remuneration, and whether agreement will meet or exceed NES and Award minimum
  • Identify key clauses needed: confidentiality, IP assignment, restraint of trade, garden leave
2

Drafting & execution

Drafting
  • Draft employment agreement — role, remuneration, hours, leave, IP, confidentiality, termination notice, post-employment restraints
  • Restraint of trade: must be reasonable in scope, geography, and duration — cascade clause recommended
  • Fair Work Act 2009: ensure no contravention of general protections, adverse action provisions
  • Executive contracts: include bonus KPIs, equity vesting, termination payment mechanics
  • Arrange execution; provide written notice of Fair Work Information Statement to new employee

Workflow — Will
1

Instructions & testamentary capacity

Foundation
  • Confirm testamentary capacity — does client understand nature and effect of a will, extent of property, claims of family?
  • Satisfy yourself client is not under undue influence — meet alone where possible
  • Obtain full picture: assets (real property, bank accounts, superannuation, shares, business interests), liabilities, dependants
  • Note: superannuation does not form part of the estate — binding death benefit nomination required separately
2

Drafting & execution

Drafting
  • Draft will — executor, trustee (if minors involved), specific bequests, residuary estate, guardianship of minor children
  • Consider testator's family maintenance claims — adequate provision for eligible persons (Succession Act 2006 NSW Pt 3.2)
  • Execution: testator signs in presence of two adult witnesses; witnesses must not be beneficiaries
  • Safekeeping: advise client to store original in a secure location; offer safe custody if firm practice
  • Advise on reviewing the will after major life events — marriage, divorce, birth of children, acquisition of property

Workflow — Power of Attorney
1

Instructions & type of POA

Foundation
  • Determine type: general POA (ceases on incapacity), enduring POA (survives incapacity), or limited/specific POA
  • Confirm donor has capacity; satisfy yourself no undue influence
  • Identify attorney(s) and scope of authority — financial decisions, property transactions, personal/lifestyle decisions (enduring guardianship is separate)
2

Drafting & registration

Completion
  • Use prescribed form under Powers of Attorney Act 2003 NSW; include any limitations or conditions
  • Execution: donor signs before an authorised witness (solicitor, barrister, JP, notary); witness certifies capacity
  • If POA will be used for land transactions — must be registered with NSW LRS; prepare registration documents
  • Provide certified copies to attorney; advise attorney of duties and responsibilities

Workflow — Family law (general intake)
1

Initial consultation & safety check

Foundation
  • Conflict check — confirm you do not act or have not acted for the other party in the matter
  • Safety screening: domestic violence, family violence orders, children at risk — document carefully; refer to DV services if needed
  • Confirm jurisdiction: Federal Circuit and Family Court of Australia (FCFCOA) for family law matters
  • Identify issues: property settlement, parenting arrangements, child support, spousal maintenance, divorce
2

Pre-action procedure & negotiation

Negotiation
  • Family law pre-action procedure (property): write to other party; attempt genuine negotiation before filing
  • Parenting: attempt family dispute resolution (FDR) with accredited FDR practitioner; obtain s60I certificate before filing (exceptions: urgency, DV, child abuse)
  • Financial disclosure: exchange s13A Financial Statement; mutual duty of full and frank disclosure
  • Consider consent orders or parenting plan if parties can reach agreement
📁Family law folder
Workflow — Transfer of property (inter-party / gift)
1

Instructions & stamp duty advice

Foundation
  • Confirm parties and nature of transfer: commercial sale, gift, family arrangement, relationship breakdown
  • Stamp duty: NSW transfer duty applies on market value regardless of consideration paid — advise client
  • Exemptions: matrimonial home exemption, deceased estate, certain family farm transfers — check eligibility
  • CGT: advise transferor on CGT implications — main residence exemption, cost base, timing
2

Transfer & registration

Completion
  • Prepare transfer instrument; obtain valuation if required for duty assessment
  • Lodge duty assessment with Revenue NSW; pay duty; obtain duty endorsement
  • Lodge transfer at NSW LRS via PEXA or manual lodgement; pay registration fee
  • Confirm title updated in Land Registry; report to parties; close file

Workflow — company set-up & incorporation
1

Instructions & structure advice

Foundation
  • Conflict check; obtain instructions; confirm who gives instructions if multiple founders
  • Advise on entity type: Pty Ltd, public company, incorporated association, trust — different liability, tax, and governance consequences
  • Confirm number and identity of directors and shareholders; confirm share structure (classes, voting rights, dividend rights)
  • Discuss need for shareholders agreement alongside constitution — who has control, deadlock resolution, exit mechanisms

2

Incorporation & ASIC lodgement

Registration
  • Lodge Form 201 (Incorporating a Company) with ASIC via ASIC Connect; pay incorporation fee
  • Obtain ACN and certificate of registration; confirm company name availability
  • Register ABN; register for GST if turnover expected above $75,000; register for PAYG withholding
  • Prepare company registers: members register, directors register, share register, options register

3

Constitution & shareholders agreement

Governance
  • Draft bespoke constitution or adopt replaceable rules (s135 Corporations Act); lodge with ASIC if adopting constitution
  • Draft shareholders agreement — board composition, reserved matters, pre-emptive rights, drag-along, tag-along, deadlock
  • Issue shares — prepare share certificates; record issue in share register; lodge ASIC notification (Form 484)
  • Hold first directors' meeting — appoint officers, open bank account, adopt common seal if required

Preliminary & due diligence

Investigation

Workflow
1

Intake, contract review & pre-exchange advice

Foundation
  • Conflict check; file opening; cost agreement
  • Review Contract for Sale of Land 2026 NSW — title, vendor disclosure docs, easements, zoning
  • Review s10.7 planning certificate — zoning, heritage, bushfire, contamination, flooding
  • Advise purchaser on terms, special conditions, and any concerns prior to exchange
  • Negotiate amendments; prepare counter-schedule if required
  • Finance condition: confirm approval status; advise on risks of exchanging without finance approval
  • Check surcharge purchaser duty liability if applicable (foreign person — s104JA Duties Act 1997 NSW)
📁 (a) 2026 Contract (b) eCOS (c) Special Conditions Vendor Disclosure
2

Exchange

Binding contract
  • Arrange exchange — in person, post, or through agent; 66W or 66U if cooling-off waiver needed
  • Confirm deposit paid into vendor's solicitor trust account (usually 10%)
  • Diarise: cooling-off expiry, finance deadline, special condition deadlines, settlement date
  • Notify lender/broker; forward contract to bank for mortgage instructions
  • Rural land: check water licence transfer obligations (special conditions 5.2/5.3)

3

Requisitions & pre-settlement searches

Investigation
  • Issue requisitions on title; raise any title defects, mortgages, or encumbrances
  • Order searches: title search, rates certificate, water certificate, land tax clearance certificate
  • PEXA workspace: create or join; invite mortgagee and vendor's solicitor
  • Stamp duty: confirm liability; lodge with Revenue NSW via PEXA
  • Obtain and execute incoming mortgage documents from bank
  • Rural land: confirm rural requisitions and lodgement exception form if applicable

Settlement (PEXA) & post-settlement

Completion

  • Sign and certify transfer electronically in PEXA; authorise settlement
  • Monitor workspace — settlement occurs automatically when all parties ready
  • Notify client; confirm keys/access arranged with agent
  • Confirm title registration in Land Registry Services; report to client; close file

Pre-settlement checklist

Workflow — stage by stage
1

Pre-litigation assessment & letter of demand

Foundation
  • Conflict check; file opening; cost agreement — advise client on costs exposure upfront
  • Assess limitation period — s14 Limitation Act 1969 NSW: 6 years for contract/debt claims from when cause of action accrued
  • Identify cause(s) of action; assess strength of evidence; advise on prospects and ADR alternatives
  • Issue letter of demand with 14–28 days to respond; outline consequences of non-compliance
📁 Letter of Demand Procedure notes
2

Commencing proceedings

Pleadings
  • Select correct court: Local Court (up to $100k), District Court (up to $750k), Supreme Court (unlimited)
  • Draft Statement of Claim — plead each element of the cause of action precisely; avoid embarrassment
  • File and serve — personal service as default; substituted service if required (Form 20 motion + affidavit)
  • Attend first directions hearing — timetable, hearing estimate, case management orders
📁 [2] SOC folder
3

Defendant response & interlocutory steps

Defence
  • File Notice of Appearance (defendant); file Defence within time; consider cross-claim
  • Interlocutory applications — injunctions, substituted service, security for costs, discovery
  • Discovery: prepare list of documents; review opponent's; raise objections where privileged
  • Subpoenas to third parties; expert evidence if technical issues in dispute
  • Reply promptly to any requests for information from the other party
📁 [3] Defendant Response Reply to other party
4

Pre-trial preparation

Preparation
  • Prepare court book / tender bundle — paginate and index all documents chronologically
  • Brief counsel if instructing — prepare detailed solicitor's brief with chronology and issues list
  • Prepare opening address outline and closing submissions outline
  • Prepare witness examination (EIC) and cross-examination question outlines
  • Attend any final case management conference or court-ordered mediation
📁 [4] Pre-trial folder
5

Court hearing

Hearing
  • Arrive 20 minutes early — confirm all parties present; take final settlement instructions from client
  • State appearance: full name, firm, party you represent — spell your name
  • Open with the order you are seeking; use "I submit…" not "I think…"
  • Bow entering and leaving; remain at bar table until excused; no phone at bar table
  • If you don't know the answer — say so and offer to provide it at recess
📁 [5] Court Hearing folder
6

Settlement & enforcement

Completion
  • If settled: draft or review Deed of Settlement and Release; obtain execution by all parties
  • If judgment: obtain sealed copy; advise client on appeal options and timeline
  • Enforcement: Writ of Execution, garnishee orders, examination of judgment debtor as to means
  • Costs assessment if costs in dispute; close file; issue final invoice

Career Notes

Soft skills & craft

From your Notion PLT notes — client interviewing, writing, advocacy, ethics, and professional growth

Before the meeting Prep

  • Always run a conflict check before accepting — confirm no existing client on the other side
  • Do your homework — familiarise yourself with the client's background before they arrive
  • Assess risk level: high-risk or low-risk? Consider due diligence searches
  • Assess sophistication — adjust communication depth accordingly
  • Meet in person for the first meeting if possible — pick up on the client's vibe; match their energy

Greeting & opening First impression

  • Always meet clients in the conference room — not your desk (confidentiality)
  • Introduce yourself with first and last name, always
  • Start with small talk; transition efficiently to the matter at hand
  • Observe how senior associates dress and interact — mirror that until you find your own style
  • Be transparent about fees at the outset — how the firm charges and for what
  • Never give legal advice in the first meeting until you have reviewed all documents

During the interview Questioning

  • Use open questions for context; closed questions for drilling down on specific facts
  • Avoid double-barrelled questions — ask one thing at a time
  • When asking potentially sensitive questions, explain why you need that information first
  • Stay objective — don't believe everything the client says; verify against documents
  • Don't ask "do you have any questions?" — ask "do you have any concerns about [specific issue]?"
  • Never let the client leave without knowing exactly what happens next
Communication problems account for up to 40% of claims against lawyers. This is a service industry.

File notes Documentation

  • Capture: Who, When, What, Why, How — including related parties and apparently irrelevant detail
  • Record the client's exact words — for future affidavit drafting, use their phrasing
  • Note key dates — court dates, document deadlines, next steps agreed
  • Structure chronologically with headings — make it easy to brief a senior quickly
  • For high-risk clients: always follow up in writing after the meeting

Writing for clients Plain language

  • Write so the client can understand — plain language, clear and concise
  • Always tell the client WHAT to do and WHEN
  • Always reference their instructions: "You have instructed me that…"
  • If the client needs to sign: bring them in and explain — don't just post documents cold
  • If posting: print three copies; mark every signature and initials point clearly

Writing for the court & other lawyers Formal

  • Writing is your reputation — treat every document as a reflection of your professional standing
  • Never include your name as contact unless comfortable with it being shown in court
  • Always include GST in any cost figures
  • Align and format documents properly — sloppy formatting undermines credibility

Email conventions Correspondence

  • Study your senior associate's email style — adopt their structure and tone
  • One issue = one paragraph
  • High-risk clients: write advice as a letter and attach — not in the email body
  • Hard copy letter: "Enclosed". Word document: "Attached"
  • CC order: most relevant to the matter first
  • Do not get personally invested — control your tone; keep client emotions out of correspondence

Standard letter phrases Templates

  • "We act for…"
  • "We are instructed that…"
  • "We have instructions to…"
  • "We trust the matter can be resolved amicably…"
Recommended reading: Point Made — consistently praised by practitioners for good legal writing.

Court basics Protocol

  • Arrive 20 minutes before listing — confirm all parties; take final settlement instructions
  • Bow entering and leaving the courtroom; no phone; do not react to what is said in court
  • State appearance: "Your Honour, I appear for the plaintiff. My name is [full name]." — spell your name
  • Remain at bar table until excused; remain silent when an oath is taken
  • Address the judge as "Your Honour"; address the Registrar directly

Submissions Advocacy

  • Open with the order you are seeking and what you want the court to do
  • Use court language: "I submit…" not "I think…"
  • Refer to solicitors as "my friend"; barristers as "my learned friend"
  • Close: "Those are my submissions unless there is anything else I can assist Your Honour with"
  • Never mislead a judge or jury — your role is to help find the truth
  • If you don't know: "I don't know but I will find out at recess, Your Honour"
Preparation is almost everything. Knowing your client and the facts cold fills in a lot of the "still learning" gaps.

What a "mention" is Listing types

  • A brief administrative hearing to update the court on the status of proceedings
  • Be ready: what has been done since last time, and what does the court need to order next?
  • Common outputs: evidence timetable, referral to mediation, hearing date fixed

Core ethical duties Conduct

  • Be rules-based — follow the conduct rules in real life, not just in theory
  • Duty to disclose to the court — remain honest; your career and your firm depend on it
  • If client instructs against your advice: document everything — you don't own their problem
  • If the relationship needs to end: let them terminate you, not the other way around
  • Never waive your bill — client must pay before file transferred to another solicitor

Managing client risk Risk management

  • Google the client before the first meeting — always
  • Distinguish high-risk from low-risk early — conduct searches, assess patterns
  • Always confirm important advice in writing — if no documentation, you never gave that advice
  • Know who has power in your firm — equity partners own the firm

Billing awareness Firm structure

  • Equity partner — do not underestimate them; they own the firm
  • Salaried partner — approx. $1,000–1,200/hr
  • Special counsel / Senior associate — approx. $600/hr
  • Associate — $400–500/hr · Solicitor — $300–400/hr · Paralegal — approx. $200/hr
  • Record time accurately — know whether your firm charges hourly or flat rate

Mindset Attitude

  • Mentorship is worth its weight in gold — find a mentor inside or outside the firm
  • Preparation is almost everything — know your client and facts cold
  • Attitude is everything else — take mistakes with grace; they don't define you
  • Be excellent and irreplaceable in your area of interest — don't try to be mediocre everywhere
  • Be responsive — reply promptly, even just to say you're looking into it

Managing up Working with seniors

  • Observe senior associates and partners — watch how they dress, write, speak, and handle clients
  • Find someone you want to be in 5–10 years — ask them for coffee
  • Brief your senior clearly and concisely from the file note — make it easy to get across the matter fast
  • Seek help when needed — never bluff or guess on important matters

Craft the market values Skills

  • Attention to detail — the most important skill; errors have real consequences for real people
  • Humility inside, confidence outside — know what you don't know; project assurance to clients
  • Curiosity — ask why, not just what; understand the law behind the rule
  • Self-discipline — no one will chase you; own your deadlines
  • Adaptability — every client, every matter, every supervisor is different
  • Cross-cultural awareness — adjust communication style to the person in front of you
Every young lawyer makes mistakes. Taking them with grace — "Thank you for explaining, Your Honour. May we have a brief recess?" — makes all the difference.
